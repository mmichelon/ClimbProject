Climbing Equipment

Indoor Climbing Equipment

Essential Bouldering gear for Indoor and Outdoor climbing.

Climbing Shoe

These fit smaller than normal shoes, with full rubber soles and toes. Important for using small footholds.

Chalk Bag/Boulder Bag

Chalk is used to dry your hands allowing for better ability to hold onto more difficult holds.

Toprope/lead climbing

Climbing Harness

Harnesses allow for safe free climbing. The climber ties into the front of the harness, while the belayer is clipped in the other side.

Belay Device Or Gregory

A person will clip the belay device to their harness. Once the climber is on the wall this device allows you to catch them if they fall, and lower them.

Outdoor Climbing Equipment

All indoor climbing equipment is also used outdoors, however additional gear is needed to climb safley outdoors.

Climbing Helmet

Lightweight helmets to protect against falling rocks, and hitting your head when outdoor climbing.

Locking Carabiners

Saftey equipment to connect other pieces of gear, be it harness to belay device, or harness to daisy chain.

Climbing Rope

A climber will attach this to their harness, and a belayer will addach the other end, the climber puts the rope through carabiners attached to either cams, nuts, or bolts.

Cam

A device placed into cracks or pockets so that pulling on the axle forces the cams to spread farther apart. This secures the climber if they call, and are easily removable.

Nut

A device placed in small cracks, that secure the climber to the wall. These are generally used when a cam is too large.

Daisy Chain

Used to either secure the belayer to the ground, or hold the climber off a bold or gear in order to place more gear or rest.

Crash Pad

In outdoor bouldering these are placed below the route in case the climber falls. Most climbing gyms have this built into their floor.

Ascender

A divice used to go up a rope, this is usefull when carring equipment up a large multi-pitch climb. Also used in mountaineering on sleep slopes.
